I work at the intersection of public finance, social protection, urban governance, and climate-resilient service delivery, combining data analysis, field research, and institutional diagnostics to support evidence-informed policymaking.

About Me

I am a public policy researcher with training in economics and applied policy analysis. My academic background includes a Master’s in Public Policy from IIT Tirupati and a Bachelor’s in Economics (Honours) from Sri Sathya Sai Institute of Higher Learning, Prasanthi Nilayam.

My interests focus on how public institutions design, finance, and implement programs that shape economic security, service delivery, and welfare outcomes. I am particularly interested in social protection systems, municipal finance, and the use of data and evaluation frameworks to improve policy effectiveness.

I have hands-on research experience through my internship at the Institute for Social and Economic Change (ISEC), Bengaluru, where I contributed to large-scale climate advisory programs, policy reports, and monitoring frameworks impacting millions of beneficiaries.
